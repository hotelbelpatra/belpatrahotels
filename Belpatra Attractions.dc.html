Nearby Attractions

All the wonders of Orchha — within walking distance from Belpatra

Ram Raja Mandir

3 min walk

The divine crown of Orchha — the only temple in India where Lord Ram is worshipped as a King with police salutes and a state flag. A must-visit for every pilgrim.

🕐 Open: 8 AM – 12 PM & 4 PM – 8 PM · Free entry

Chaturbhuj Temple

5 min walk

Built in the 16th century to house Ram Raja's idol, this towering cross-shaped temple stands 344 feet tall with stunning architecture and panoramic views from the top.

🕐 Open: Sunrise to Sunset · Nominal entry fee

Orchha Fort Complex

10 min walk

The grand 16th-century Bundela fort houses Jahangir Mahal, Raja Mahal and Rai Praveen Mahal. A sprawling complex of palaces with intricate frescoes and stunning courtyards.

🕐 Open: 8 AM – 6 PM · ASI entry fee applies

Jahangir Mahal

12 min walk

A 17th-century guest palace built to welcome Emperor Jahangir — a masterpiece of Mughal-Rajput fusion architecture with magnificent carved balconies and towers.

🕐 Open: 8 AM – 6 PM · Entry included in Fort ticket

River Betwa & Ghats

15 min walk

The sacred Betwa river flows alongside Orchha, offering beautiful sunrise and sunset views, peaceful river ghats, boat rides and cenotaphs lining the banks.

🕐 Open all day · Best at sunrise & sunset

Laxminarayan Temple

20 min walk

A 17th-century temple famous for its extraordinary wall murals depicting scenes from the Ramayana, Mahabharata and local Bundela history — an open-air art museum.

🕐 Open: 8 AM – 6 PM · Small entry fee

Plan Your Orchha Visit

👟
Wear Comfortable Shoes
Orchha is best explored on foot — cobblestone paths and temple stairs abound
🌅
Start Early
Attend morning aarti at Ram Raja Mandir (6 AM) — the most divine experience in Orchha
🧣
Dress Respectfully
Cover shoulders and knees when entering temples. Scarves available at market stalls.
📸
Photography
Photography allowed at most monuments. Check restrictions at the main temple.
